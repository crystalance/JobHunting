3. What happened

  • Classic "unlearn-then-relearn": dipped below the SFT-v3 init through steps 3–9 (down to f1_item 0.304), then recovered and rose above init from step 12 on. Normal RL exploration; it recovered — not a collapse.
  • vs SFT-v3 init (step 0), same session: f1_item ~flat (0.419→0.423, +1%), f1_row +17% (0.138→0.161), has_table +20% (0.71→0.86), n_turns held at ~6.5 (stayed efficient), score +10%. Coverage & completion up, accuracy & efficiency preserved — the intended effect.
  • Floor working: min reward = −0.10 every step (some rollouts made no table → clamped, never a local optimum). constant_fill = 0.0 at the end (no fabrication).
  • Healthy internals: entropy 0.545 (not collapsed to 0), response_length stable ~12.7k (not halving), grad_norm 0.60, ppo_kl ≈ 0.

4. Checkpoint note tradeoff

Best-by-val is step 15 (f1_item 0.442, score 0.636), but max_actor_ckpt_to_keep=2 rotated its 86 GB actor out — only step_18 and step_21 survive. This is the disk-vs-best-by-val tradeoff (each verl checkpoint is ~86 GB with optimizer; 4 kept would need ~340 GB).

In practice this is fine. Greedy val on n=14 is non-reproducible (~±0.1), so step_15's 0.442 vs step_21's 0.423 is within noise. Step_21 is the right pick anyway: highest has_table (0.857), lowest turns (6.5), competitive f1, natural endpoint. Lesson for next time: to truly keep best-by-val, snapshot (merge→15 GB HF) at each val peak rather than relying on keep-N.
