Spotlight: ws_en_023 — critic→targeted-search recovers missing rows

Query. List all articles from three sociology journals (2020–2024) whose titles contain “migrate”, “immigrate”, or their variations.

Without critic. Worker stopped after finding only 2 articles total, yielding F1row=0.034, F1item=0.064 (212 s).

With critic. The schema-only critic’s COVERAGE check flagged the gap: “Only 2 articles listed; expected several more given three journals and five years.” It then emitted targeted re-search prompts, including site:american-journal-of-cultural-sociology … migrate OR immigrate 2020..2024 and equivalents for the two other journals. The worker re-ran search and grew the answer from 2 → 9 rows, lifting F1row to 0.242 (+0.21, ×7) and F1item to 0.294 (+0.23, ×4.6).

Why it matters. The critic itself performs zero searches; it only audits structure. The gain comes from the critic’s coverage estimate (scope_estimate=10–30) forcing the worker to keep digging when it would otherwise have stopped. This is the targeted-search benefit at work: a small structural prompt unlocks 7× the recall.

Spotlight: ws_en_046 — critic recovers an answer the worker gave up on (F1row 0 → 0.74)

Query. Compile every major international tournament during Lang Ping's tenure as US Women's National Volleyball head coach (2005–2008): year, event, venue (country), roster of the final match, and final position. Covers Olympics, World Championship, World Cup, World Grand Prix, World Grand Champions Cup, and Montreux Volley Masters.

Without critic. Worker produced a table whose rows didn't match a single gold row (rosters incomplete, venue fields too coarse) → F1row=0.000, F1item=0.632 (199 s).

With critic. The schema-only critic raised 6 issues across roster blanks and venue precision, e.g. "Roster is '-' for 2006 FIVB World Grand Prix" and "Venue 'Montreux, Switzerland' includes city; prompt asks for country precision". The worker re-ran targeted roster searches per event, fixed venues, and recovered F1row=0.737 (+0.74) and F1item=0.884 (+0.25).

Why it matters. Without the audit, the worker accepted its own partial answer as complete. The critic's structural check (blank rosters, malformed venues) gave the worker a concrete to-do list that turned a zero-row-match answer into a usable one.

Spotlight: ws_en_031 — critic catches an entire missing entity (F1row 0.29 → 0.88)

Query. List every film (2010–2020) for Angelina Jolie, Nicole Kidman, Cate Blanchett, and Jodie Foster — title, character, year, awards. Exclude shorts and voice-only roles.

Without critic. Worker output 6 films for Jolie, 4 for Blanchett, some for Kidman, and silently omitted Jodie Foster entirely → F1row=0.286, F1item=0.771 (135 s).

With critic. The COVERAGE check fired with the exact diagnosis: "Jodie Foster is missing entirely", "Only 6 films listed for Angelina Jolie; likely incomplete", "Only 4 films listed for Cate Blanchett", plus an overall scope estimate (~40–50 films). The worker re-ran per-actress searches and produced a near-complete answer: F1row=0.876 (+0.59), F1item=0.968 (+0.20).

Why it matters. A schema-only audit (zero searches) noticed that one of the four required entities had no rows at all. The single sentence "Jodie Foster is missing entirely" was enough to triple the per-actress yield. This is the cheapest failure mode the critic catches: silent under-coverage.
